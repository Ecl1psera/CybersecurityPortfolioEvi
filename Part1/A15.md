# Change Healthcare cyberattack

Source: https://hyperproof.io/resource/understanding-the-change-healthcare-breach/

The Change Healthcare attack was a ransomware attack that prevented the use of their
electronic payments service. This attack lead to a large distruption to various
healthcare providers which took months to recover from and lead to investigations 
into the companies HIPPA complience policies.


# Ticketmaster data breach

Source: https://www.huntress.com/threat-library/data-breach/ticketmaster-data-breach

560M customer records were leaked by a group through the explotation of the services 
third party integration. They were able to gain access to customers names, emails,
and payment details.

# AT&T data leak 

Source: https://www.mozillafoundation.org/en/privacynotincluded/articles/att-had-a-huge-data-breach-heres-what-you-need-to-know/

The AT&T data breach was due to the lack of security in a third party cloud storage
service. This resulted in the exfiltration of millions of records including calls
and texts that were made to and from AT&T phones.

# Microsoft Midnight Blizzard attack

Source: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2024/10/29/midnight-blizzard-conducts-large-scale-spear-phishing-campaign-using-rdp-files/

Russian threat actors sent highly targeted phishing emails to various high ranking 
officials containing a malicious .RDP attachments. When the attachment was opened
it would connect the victims computer to the actors server providing them complete
access to the victims computer.

# Snowflake customer data breaches

Source: https://cloudsecurityalliance.org/blog/2025/05/07/unpacking-the-2024-snowflake-data-breach

The threat actor used previous data gathered through infostealers to log into 
customers accounts and access stored data. This lead to $2m in extortions from AT&T,
Ticketmaster, and Santander.
