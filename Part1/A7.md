# RSA Asymmetric Encryption

RSA Encryption uses private/public key pairs to encrypt and decrypt messages.
The use of private/public keys avoids the issue of man in the middle attacks
since any attacks will not have the means to easily decrypt any messages sent 
by the public key holder. Along with this, the sender can use their private
key to sign their messages to verify they are the sender by generating a hash
from their private key and the message, the reciever can then use the
corresponding public key to create a copy of the hash to verify the signature.

RSA gets its strength through the difficulty thats required to compute the 
prime factors that make up the modulus to produce the keys.
